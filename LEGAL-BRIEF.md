## 1. MICHIGAN STATE LAW

### Michigan Money Transmission Act (MCL 487.1001 et seq.)

**Verdict: Likely exempt.**

- Regulates "receiving money or monetary value for transmission"
- Closed-loop, non-redeemable tokens are generally exempt (similar to gift cards)
- DIFS (Dept of Insurance & Financial Services) has not specifically addressed community tokens
- **Key requirement:** NO cash redemption, NO use outside the cooperative
- If tokens ever become convertible to USD → triggers regulation immediately

**Cite:** MCL 487.1003(k); DIFS Bulletin 2018-01

### Michigan Cooperative Laws

- **Michigan Cooperative Corporation Act (MCL 450.98 et seq.)** — allows formation for mutual benefit
- **Michigan Cooperative Associations Act (MCL 450.2701 et seq.)** — alternative path
- File articles of incorporation with LARA, pay filing fee ($10-50), adopt bylaws
- Members limited to participants (20-50)
- No profit distribution beyond patronage refunds
- Provides liability protection

### No Specific Local Currency Prohibition

- Michigan has **no statute** specifically regulating local currencies, scrip, or barter systems
- Historical precedent: Michigan allowed company scrip in mining towns
- Recent crypto legislation (HB 4102-4107, 2019) targets cryptocurrency kiosks — does NOT apply to offline, mesh-based tokens

## 2. FEDERAL LAW

### IRS Barter Exchange Rules (IRC §6045)

**This is the biggest compliance obligation.**

- Any organized barter exchange must file **Form 1099-B** for each member
- Report **fair market value** of all exchanges
- GPT-4.1 says no de minimis threshold; Perplexity says $600 threshold per participant
- **Conservative approach:** Report ALL transactions, let members declare income
- Barter income is taxable as ordinary income at fair market value upon receipt

**Cite:** IRC §6045(c)(3); [IRS Barter Exchanges](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/barter-exchanges)

### FinCEN / Bank Secrecy Act

**Verdict: Not triggered (if structured correctly).**

- Closed-loop, non-convertible value systems are NOT money transmission
- Same category as loyalty points, airline miles, store credit
- **Key guidance:** FinCEN FIN-2019-G001 (May 2019) — convertible virtual currency guidance explicitly carves out non-convertible, closed-loop systems

**Cite:** 31 CFR §1010.100(ff); [FinCEN CVC Guidance 2019](https://www.fincen.gov/sites/default/files/2019-05/FinCEN%20Guidance%20CVC%20FINAL%20508.pdf)

### Key Case Law

- **United States v. Bernard von NotHaus** (850 F.3d 1135, 11th Cir. 2017) — "Liberty Dollar" criminalized because it was marketed as a **substitute for U.S. currency**. Beaver Island Credits avoid this by being explicitly NOT currency.
- **BerkShares (MA)** — operates legally as scrip under state banking exemptions
- **Ithaca Hours (NY)** — treated as barter, not currency, for decades
- **People v. Detroit Barter Club (1989, MI)** — barter club upheld as legal (Grok cite, unpublished)

## 3. RECOMMENDED LEGAL STRUCTURE

### Entity: Michigan Cooperative Corporation

**Why cooperative over LLC:**
- Fits mutual benefit purpose (not profit-driven)
- Democratic governance (one member, one vote)
- Patronage-based returns (not equity distribution)
- Avoids "investment contract" / securities classification
- Liability protection for members

### Formation Steps

1. Choose name (e.g., "Beaver Island Veterans Cooperative Association")
2. File Articles of Incorporation with Michigan LARA ($10-50)
3. Draft bylaws (membership, voting, token issuance rules, dispute resolution)
4. Hold organizational meeting, elect officers
5. Open cooperative bank account (for any fiat expenses — insurance, legal fees)
6. Register as barter exchange with IRS if required

### Critical Rules to Maintain Exemption

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| **No cash redemption** | Avoids money transmission classification |
| **Members only** | Closed-loop, no public access |
| **No marketing as "currency"** | Avoids von NotHaus precedent |
| **Real asset backing** | Every credit traces to real goods/services |
| **Transaction records** | IRS compliance, 5+ year retention |
| **Annual 1099-B filing** | Required for organized barter exchanges |
| **"Not legal tender" disclaimer** | On all materials, in bylaws |

## 4. CHARLEVOIX COUNTY / PAINE TOWNSHIP

### Local Requirements

- **No known local ordinances** regulating barter systems or community currencies
- **Business license:** Charlevoix County Equalization Department — check for co-op exemptions ($50-200 typical fee)
- **Paine Township:** Contact township clerk for any local registration requirements

### Radio/Mesh Equipment Zoning

- **FCC Part 15:** Unlicensed low-power devices (<1W on ISM bands) — **no license required**
- **LoRa 915 MHz:** Falls under Part 15 unlicensed rules
- **Antenna restrictions:** Charlevoix County Zoning Ordinance Article 4 — accessory structures <35ft residential
- **Practical:** Small LoRa antennas on existing structures (sheds, roofs) unlikely to trigger any review
- **If mounting visible tower antenna:** May need site plan review

## 5. RISK ASSESSMENT

### What Triggers Federal Attention

| Trigger | Risk Level | Mitigation |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Tokens become convertible to USD | 🔴 HIGH | Prohibit in bylaws, enforce strictly |
| Non-members use the system | 🔴 HIGH | Membership-only, access controlled |
| Marketed as "currency" or "money" | 🔴 HIGH | Always call it "barter credits" or "exchange tokens" |
| Transactions exceed $10K aggregate | 🟡 MEDIUM | Track and report via 1099-B |
| Failure to report barter income | 🟡 MEDIUM | File 1099-B annually, educate members |
| Illegal goods exchanged | 🔴 HIGH | Strict bylaws — firewood, fish, repairs, meds only |
| Island gossip / public exposure | 🟡 MEDIUM | Opsec, need-to-know basis |

### VA Benefits Risk

**⚠️ This is the sleeper risk nobody talks about.**

- Barter income may count as "countable income" under 38 CFR §3.271
- Could affect means-tested VA pensions and benefits
- Fair market value of goods/services received could offset benefits
- **Mitigation:** Structure as co-op expense reimbursements rather than "income"
- **Action:** Each participating veteran should consult their VA benefits counselor

### How Existing Systems Stay Legal

- BerkShares: Non-convertible scrip, partnership with local banks, transparent
- Ithaca Hours: Treated as barter, no MSB registration, strict local use
- Time banks: Often structured as 501(c)(3) nonprofits
- **Common thread:** Transparency, non-convertibility, community framing, tax compliance
