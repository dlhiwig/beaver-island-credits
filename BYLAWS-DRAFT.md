# BYLAWS OF ARAMORE VETERANS COOPERATIVE ASSOCIATION

*A Michigan Cooperative Corporation under MCL 450.98 et seq.*

---

## 1. NAME, PURPOSE, AND REGISTERED OFFICE

**1.1 Name:** Beaver Island Veterans Cooperative Association ("the Cooperative").

**1.2 Purpose:** The Cooperative is organized exclusively for the mutual benefit of its members, who are United States military veterans and their immediate families, to facilitate a closed-loop barter exchange system known as "Beaver Island Credits" on Beaver Island, Paine Township, Charlevoix County, Michigan. Purposes include:
- Promoting economic self-sufficiency and mutual aid among members
- Facilitating exchange of goods and services (firewood, fish, repairs, medical supplies) using Beaver Island Credits
- Supporting well-being and community integration of veterans and families

**1.3 Registered Office:** Located in Paine Township, Charlevoix County, Michigan, as designated in Articles of Incorporation.

---

## 2. MEMBERSHIP

**2.1 Eligibility:** Open to:
- (a) Honorably discharged United States military veterans
- (b) Immediate family members (spouse, domestic partner, children, legal dependents) of such veterans

**2.2 Application Process:** Written application, proof of eligibility, written agreement to abide by Bylaws. Board of Directors approves/denies by majority vote.

**2.3 Maximum Members:** No fewer than 20 and no more than 50 at any time.

**2.4 Removal:** For cause (Bylaws violation, fraud, detrimental conduct) by 2/3 vote of Board, after notice and opportunity to be heard.

---

## 3. MEETINGS

**3.1 Annual Meeting:** Each year, time/place set by Board. Elections, reports, business.

**3.2 Special Meetings:** Called by Board or written request of 10%+ of members.

**3.3 Notice:** Written notice 10-60 days before meeting, stating date, time, place, purpose.

**3.4 Quorum:** 50% of members.

**3.5 Voting:** One member, one vote. No proxy voting.

---

## 4. BOARD OF DIRECTORS

**4.1 Composition:** 3-5 members, elected for staggered two-year terms. May serve consecutive terms.

**4.2 Duties:** Manage affairs/property, establish policies, appoint committees (including Token Issuance Committee), oversee legal compliance.

**4.3 Removal:** With or without cause, by 2/3 vote of membership at a meeting called for that purpose.

**4.4 Vacancies:** Filled by majority vote of remaining directors until next annual meeting.

---

## 5. TOKEN ISSUANCE COMMITTEE

**5.1 Composition:** 3 members (at least 1 director), appointed annually by Board.

**5.2 Authority:**
- Exclusive authority to issue ("mint") Beaver Island Credits
- Credits only issued when fully backed by real, tangible goods or services
- Must maintain detailed records of all issuances
- Total credits in circulation must not exceed value of goods/services held or committed

**5.3 Audit:** Annual audit by independent member or third party. Summary report to membership.

---

## 6. TRANSACTION RULES

**6.1 Approved Goods/Services:** Pre-approved by Board. Includes firewood, fish, repairs, medical supplies, and other items supporting member welfare.

**6.2 Prohibited Items:**
- Anything illegal under federal, state, or local law
- Alcohol, tobacco, controlled substances, firearms
- Anything jeopardizing Cooperative's legal status

**6.3 Dispute Resolution:** Written submission to Board → investigation → decision. Appeal to membership (majority vote is final).

---

## 7. NON-CONVERTIBILITY CLAUSE

**7.1** Beaver Island Credits are NOT, and shall never be, convertible to United States Dollars (USD) or any other legal tender, nor to any cryptocurrency or digital asset. Any attempt to convert, sell, or exchange Beaver Island Credits for legal tender or cryptocurrency is strictly prohibited and grounds for immediate expulsion.

---

## 8. "NOT LEGAL TENDER" DISCLAIMER

**8.1 Required Disclaimer (must appear on ALL cooperative materials):**

> **"Beaver Island Credits are NOT legal tender, NOT convertible to U.S. Dollars or any currency, and NOT cryptocurrency. They are solely for barter among members of the Beaver Island Veterans Cooperative Association."**

---

## 9. RECORD-KEEPING

**9.1 Transaction Logs:** Accurate, contemporaneous logs of all transactions (parties, date, nature, value).

**9.2 Retention:** Minimum 5 years.

**9.3 IRS Compliance:** Comply with all tax reporting requirements including IRS Form 1099-B.

---

## 10. VA BENEFITS ADVISORY

**10.1** All members must acknowledge in writing that barter transactions may affect VA benefits eligibility. Cooperative does not provide legal or tax advice. Members encouraged to consult qualified professionals.

---

## 11. DISSOLUTION

**11.1** Upon dissolution:
- (a) Outstanding credits redeemed by distribution of available goods/services proportional to balances
- (b) Remaining assets distributed to 501(c)(3) organization(s) as determined by membership

---

## 12. AMENDMENTS

**12.1** Bylaws amended only by 2/3 vote of entire membership at a meeting with 30+ days advance written notice of proposed amendments.
