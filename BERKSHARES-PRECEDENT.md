# BerkShares — Legal Precedent for Beaver Island Credits

**The most successful community currency in the United States. Operating since 2006.**

## Overview
- **Location:** Berkshire County, western Massachusetts (~130,000 residents)
- **Launch:** September 29, 2006
- **Operator:** BerkShares Local Inc. (501(c)(3) nonprofit)
- **Circulation:** ~140,000 BerkShares active at any time; 10M+ total issued since 2006
- **Businesses:** 300+ participating (down from 400+ peak)
- **Exchange points:** 6 bank branches across 2 regional banks (e.g., Lee Bank)

## How It Works
1. **Issuance:** Backed 1:1 by USD held in reserve at participating banks
2. **Exchange:** Users buy BerkShares at bank branches ($10 USD = B$10)
3. **Circulation:** Accepted as cash at participating local businesses
4. **Redemption:** Convert back to USD
   - Businesses: 1:1 with 1.5% fee
   - Individuals: Small amounts up to ~$5 with $0.50 fee
5. **Membership:** $25/year (or 25 BerkShares) for full participation

## Governance (Ostrom-Aligned)
- Clear boundaries (Berkshire County region)
- Congruence with local needs
- Collective choice (board/community input)
- Monitoring via banks/business directory
- Graduated sanctions (fees/exclusion)
- Conflict resolution through nonprofit admin

## Recent Developments (2025-2026)
- Formed **BerkShares Local Inc.** (state-chartered nonprofit) — enables tax-deductible donations
- **Digital payment platform** in development (mobile app/digital wallet)
- Paper notes remain in parallel use
- Annual community meetings continue

## Why This Matters for Beaver Island Credits
BerkShares proves:
1. **Legal viability** — 20 years of operation with no legal challenges
2. **Bank partnership model** — community banks handle exchange/redemption
3. **501(c)(3) structure** — enables donations and grants
4. **1:1 USD backing** — simplest, most trusted approach
5. **Physical + digital** — paper notes now transitioning to app
6. **Scale** — works in region of 130K people; Beaver Island is much smaller but same principles apply

## Key Differences for Beaver Island
| Aspect | BerkShares | Beaver Island Credits |
|--------|------------|----------------------|
| Population | ~130,000 | ~700 (seasonal: 2,000+) |
| Banks | 2 regional banks, 6 branches | No local bank (nearest: Charlevoix) |
| Infrastructure | Full internet/cell | Limited — needs LoRa mesh |
| Structure | 501(c)(3) nonprofit | Michigan Cooperative Corporation |
| Backing | 1:1 USD reserve | Cooperative membership + local goods |
| Digital | App in development | Offline-first (LoRa mesh ledger) |

## Implications for Our Design
1. **Consider 1:1 USD backing** — BerkShares' simplicity is its strength
2. **Bank partnership unlikely** — no bank on Beaver Island, need alternative (credit union? Charlevoix bank branch?)
3. **Physical notes with local heroes** — powerful community identity tool
4. **Membership model** — $25/year is proven and reasonable
5. **Anti-counterfeiting** — even paper currency needs security features
6. **Nonprofit vs Co-op** — BerkShares uses 501(c)(3); we chose Michigan Cooperative Corporation. Both work, different tax treatment.

## Comparison to Other Community Currency Systems

### vs. LETS (Local Exchange Trading Systems)
- BerkShares is **more formalized/centralized** (nonprofit + bank involvement) vs. grassroots/volunteer LETS
- LETS uses pure mutual credit (zero-sum ledger, no backing); BerkShares is **USD-backed** (not true mutual credit — issuance tied to USD deposits, not mutual promises)
- Both emphasize local recirculation and social bonds

### vs. Sardex (Sardinia, Italy)
- Sardex is **B2B-focused**, larger scale (~4,000 businesses, higher volume), professional platform with credit limits/monitoring
- BerkShares is more **consumer/retail-oriented**, smaller, paper-based (though digitizing), with stronger cultural/environmental framing

### Game Theory Analysis
- Like Beaver Island Credits/Sardex/LETS, sustains cooperation via **high δ** (repeated local interactions, reputation, sanctions via fees/exclusion)
- The **1:1 USD peg reduces volatility/defection risk** (no inflation speculation)
- Fees/punishments deter hoarding or exit abuse
- Beaver Island's small population (~700) actually *increases* δ — everyone knows everyone, defection is costly

### Where Beaver Island Credits Fits
| System | Backing | Scale | Focus | Digital |
|--------|---------|-------|-------|---------|
| LETS | Mutual credit (zero-sum) | Small/grassroots | P2P | Ledger-based |
| BerkShares | 1:1 USD reserve | Regional (130K pop) | Consumer/retail | Transitioning |
| Sardex | B2B credit limits | Large (4K businesses) | Business-to-business | Platform |
| **Beaver Island** | **Co-op membership + goods** | **Micro (700 pop)** | **Community/veteran** | **Offline-first (LoRa)** |

BerkShares is closer to our vision than pure LETS (due to backing and nonprofit structure) but shares the mutual-aid ethos. Key strategic question: **should Beaver Island Credits be USD-backed (like BerkShares) or goods-backed (like original design)?** USD backing is simpler and more trusted but requires bank partnership.
